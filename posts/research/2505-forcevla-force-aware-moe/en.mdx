## TL;DR

VLA models lack force sensing, limiting contact-rich manipulation. **ForceVLA** solves this with an **MoE-based force-vision-language fusion** that dynamically routes force information importance across task phases.

## Problem

Existing Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models depend primarily on visual and linguistic inputs, showing limitations in tasks requiring significant physical contact. Recent attempts added force data through simple concatenation, but failed to account for the varying importance of force information across different task phases. This paper addresses the challenge that contact-rich tasks like plug insertion, surface wiping, and cucumber peeling demand varying force magnitudes and directions across phases, and that precise control becomes impossible without force/tactile feedback under visual occlusion.

## Key Idea

**FVLMoE (Force-aware Vision-Language MoE)**: A sparse MoE module with 4 expert networks that dynamically routes the fusion of vision-language embeddings and 6-axis force tokens according to task phases (approach, contact, insertion, etc.). Rather than simply appending force data to inputs, it injects force information after VLM processing to preserve pretrained representations.

Key components:

- **Force tokenization**: Raw 6-axis force-torque data ($f \in \mathbb{R}^6$) linearly projected to a 2048-dim token
- **Sparse MoE**: Gating network selects top-1 expert per token, activating different experts per task phase
- **Flow-based Action Head**: Fused multimodal features injected via element-wise addition into the action generation head
- **ForceVLA-Data**: 5 tasks, 244 demonstrations, 140K synchronized vision/proprioception/force-torque timesteps

<Collapsible title="Detailed Method">

ForceVLA's training pipeline consists of three main stages.

**Stage 1 — Force Tokenization**: Raw 6-axis force-torque data ($f \in \mathbb{R}^6$) from the robot end-effector sensor is transformed into a 2048-dimensional token via a linear projection layer, matching the VLM embedding dimension.

**Stage 2 — FVLMoE Fusion**: After the VLM ($\pi_0$) processes visual-language inputs, its output embeddings and the force token are fed into the FVLMoE module. A gating network $G(x) = \text{Softmax}(W_g \cdot x)$ routes input tokens to the top-1 of 4 experts. Each expert is an independent FFN that learns specialized fusion patterns for different task phases.

**Stage 3 — Action Generation**: MoE output is injected via element-wise addition into a flow-based action head that generates continuous robot actions (joint positions/velocities). Training uses flow matching, denoising from noise to action sequences.

**Training Strategy**: The pretrained $\pi_0$ weights are frozen, and only the FVLMoE module and force tokenization layer are trained, preserving the VLM's pretrained representations while effectively integrating force information.

**Load balancing loss**: An auxiliary loss ensures balanced expert utilization, preventing token concentration on specific experts.

## Key Results

- **60.5% average success rate** across 5 contact tasks, **+23.2%p** over force-free $\pi_0$-base (37.3% → 60.5%)
- **~80% success on plug insertion**, significantly outperforming naive force concatenation ($\pi_0$-base w/ F at 40.2%)
- **90% success under visual occlusion** — demonstrating that force feedback compensates for missing visual information

## Achievements and Limitations

### What's New

- First VLA framework to fuse force information via MoE-based dynamic routing rather than simple concatenation
- Achieved 90% success under visual occlusion using force feedback alone, proving force-vision complementarity
- Demonstrated consistent improvement across 5 contact tasks with only 244 demonstrations, showing data efficiency

### Limitations

- **Estimated external wrench**: Uses estimated rather than directly measured force values, potentially limiting precision in scenarios demanding extreme haptic sensitivity
- **Expensive sensor dependency**: Experiments conducted on high-cost robotic platforms with integrated force-torque sensors (Flexiv Rizon), limiting broader accessibility
- 🤖 **Single robot platform**: Validated only on Flexiv Rizon + Dahuan gripper; transferability to other robots/grippers remains unverified
