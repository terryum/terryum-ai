## Paper Summary

This survey categorizes how Foundation Models (FM) are applied to robot manipulation into three main approaches:

1. **Vision-Language Models (VLM)** — Scene understanding and task planning
2. **Diffusion-based Policy Models** — Action generation and imitation learning
3. **Vision-Language-Action Models (VLA)** — End-to-end control

## Key Findings

- Pre-trained Vision-Language models show substantial zero-shot task understanding
- Diffusion Policy effectively models multi-modal action distributions
- Data scaling remains the biggest bottleneck, with sim-to-real transfer as a key challenge
