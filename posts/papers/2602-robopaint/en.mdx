## TL;DR

Robot learning data typically requires costly teleoperation, but RoboPaint lets humans **demonstrate tasks naturally with gloves** and converts those demonstrations into training data for any robot embodiment and any camera viewpoint. **Dex-Tactile retargeting** jointly optimizes kinematic and tactile constraints to bridge the human-robot embodiment gap, while 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) renders photorealistic training images in simulation from arbitrary views.

## Problem

Fine-tuning large VLA models for manipulation requires high-quality robot demonstration data. Teleoperation systems (ALOHA, GELLO) are slow and expensive; glove/handheld interfaces (UMI) work for grippers but fail for dexterous multi-finger hands. The fundamental challenge is the **cross-embodiment gap**: human and robot hand kinematics differ substantially, making direct transfer of human demonstrations non-trivial. Camera view diversity is also constrained — real robot setups fix camera positions, limiting data variety. Existing retargeting methods address only kinematics, missing the contact force information critical for contact-rich tasks.

## Key Idea

RoboPaint solves this in three stages: humans demonstrate naturally with instrumented gloves → Dex-Tactile retargeting maps the demonstration to robot joint states → 3DGS-reconstructed scene renders training images from arbitrary viewpoints in Isaac Sim.

**Core components:**
- **Multimodal data capture**: synchronized RGB-D cameras, RGB cameras, glove joint angles, and tactile signals in a multi-view Data-Acquisition-Room (DAR)
- **Dex-Tactile retargeting**: jointly optimizes kinematic term (fingertip position/orientation alignment) and tactile term (contact consistency via anatomical correspondence mapping), with distance-aware sigmoid weighting emphasizing active contact regions — average contact error **3.86 mm**
- **Real-Sim-Real rendering**: 3DGS model aligned to metric scale via ArUco markers → imported into Isaac Sim as USD asset → retargeted robot trajectories drive simulation → images rendered from arbitrary camera views
- **Data augmentation**: background swap (multiple DAR reconstructions) + object material swap (open-source 3D texture generation)

### Dex-Tactile Retargeting Objective

The retargeting jointly minimizes:

$$\min_{\mathbf{q}} \mathcal{L}_{kin}(\mathbf{q}) + \lambda \mathcal{L}_{tac}(\mathbf{q})$$

**Kinematic term** $\mathcal{L}_{kin}$: aligns fingertip positions and orientations of human glove with target robot links, with joint limit penalties.

**Tactile term** $\mathcal{L}_{tac}$: anatomical correspondence mapping $\phi: \mathcal{T}_{human} \to \mathcal{T}_{robot}$ projects human tactile points onto robot link surfaces. Distance-aware sigmoid weighting emphasizes active contact regions:

$$w_i = \sigma\left(-k \cdot d_i\right), \quad \mathcal{L}_{tac} = \sum_i w_i \cdot \|p_i^{robot} - \hat{p}_i\|^2$$

where $d_i$ is the spatial discrepancy and $\sigma$ is the sigmoid function.

### 3DGS Scene Reconstruction and Isaac Sim Integration

Multi-view images of the deployment environment are captured to train a 3DGS model. ArUco markers of known physical size provide metric-scale alignment. The 3DGS model is exported as a Universal Scene Description (USD) asset and imported into Isaac Sim as a static background. Retargeted robot trajectories and object poses are replayed in simulation, and image sequences are rendered from arbitrary camera positions.

### Data Augmentation

**Background swap**: multiple physical environments are reconstructed as separate 3DGS models; identical robot/object trajectories are rendered against different backgrounds to increase dataset diversity.

**Material swap**: open-source 3D generation tools produce alternative object textures; the same geometry is rendered with different materials to reduce visual domain gaps.

## Key Results

### Retargeting Success Rate

Across 10 diverse manipulation objects (boxes, bottles, bags, etc.):
- **84% average real-world replay success** (10 demonstrations per object)
- Average contact error: **3.86 mm**

### VLA Policy Performance (π0.5, with wrist camera)

| Task | Teleoperation | RoboPaint |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| Pick and Place | 100% | 70% |
| Push Cuboid | 100% | 100% |

| **Average** | **100%** | **80%** |

## Achievements and Limitations

**New achievements:**
- First pipeline generating dexterous robot hand training data from natural human demonstrations without teleoperation
- Dex-Tactile retargeting resolves the cross-embodiment gap to within 3.86 mm contact error by combining kinematic and tactile constraints
- 3DGS-based Real-Sim-Real enables arbitrary camera view, background, and material variation from a single physical demonstration

**Limitations:**
- Wrist camera proximity to 3DGS point cloud causes rendering artifacts; authors plan algorithmic improvements (author-acknowledged)
- 20%p performance gap vs teleoperation data; further widened without wrist camera (83.3% vs 46.6%)
- 🤖 Training data quality is fundamentally limited by 3DGS reconstruction fidelity — reflective, transparent, or featureless surfaces are notoriously difficult to reconstruct, potentially undermining the pipeline's generality in real-world environments
