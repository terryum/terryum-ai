## TL;DR

Teleoperation is too slow and unnatural for collecting high-quality dexterous manipulation demonstrations. DEXOP mechanically links human and robot fingers via 4-bar linkages — no actuators, no calibration — so that natural human manipulation is instantly mirrored by the robot hand with direct contact feedback. This achieves up to **8× higher throughput** than teleoperation and produces better robot policies in half the collection time.

## Problem

Dexterous robot manipulation learning remains bottlenecked by demonstration data quality and collection speed. Teleoperation forces operators to control robot hands through external interfaces, losing natural contact feel and severely limiting collection rates for complex tasks. Existing exoskeleton approaches track human hand movements via sensors and retarget them to robot joints, but this retargeting introduces kinematic mismatch that degrades data quality. The challenge is to capture the full richness of human dexterous manipulation — including contact forces and fine motor coordination — directly as robot training data.

## Core Idea

DEXOP's key innovation is **passive mechanical linkage**: 4-bar linkages physically couple human and robot finger joints with no electronics, motors, or calibration in the kinematic chain.

- **Passive exoskeleton**: Wearing DEXOP connects each human finger joint to the corresponding robot hand joint through 4-bar linkages — movement is instantaneously mirrored
- **Direct contact feedback**: Contact forces experienced by the robot hand transmit back through the linkages to the human's fingers, enabling natural tactile-guided manipulation
- **Three variants (co-design)**: DEXOP-12 (4 fingers, 12 DOF), DEXOP-9 (3 fingers, 9 DOF), DEXOP-7 (3 fingers, 7 DOF, co-designed with EyeSight hand) — linkage geometry optimized per robot hand
- **Minimal pipeline**: No retargeting or dynamics filtering needed; collected data directly feeds diffusion policy training

### Linkage Design

Each finger uses a 4-bar linkage mechanism that couples the exoskeleton input link to the robot hand output link via two connecting links, mapping human finger joint angle $\theta_{\text{human}}$ to robot finger joint angle $\theta_{\text{robot}}$ with near-linear correspondence across the working range. The thumb requires a separate rotary linkage system due to its additional rotational degrees of freedom.

The co-design process adjusts exoskeleton link geometry to match specific robot hand joint ranges and finger proportions. For DEXOP-7 (EyeSight hand), abduction joints on the index and middle fingers are removed and the thumb linkage is re-optimized to align with the EyeSight hand's actuator placement.

### Sensing and Data Collection

- **Joint angles**: Read directly from robot hand encoders — no external hand tracking required
- **Tactile**: FSR sensors on robot fingertips capture contact forces
- **Vision**: Left/right wrist cameras mounted on the exoskeleton + external cameras

The full setup pairs DEXOP with an AirExo arm exoskeleton to enable whole-arm manipulation demonstrations.

## Key Results

**Data collection throughput (Figure 6):**

- **Drilling**: DEXOP 6 completions/min vs TeleOP 0/min (teleoperation impractical)
- **Bulb installation**: DEXOP 11s vs TeleOP 86s — **8× faster**
- **Box packaging**: DEXOP 5/min vs TeleOP 0.15/min — **7× faster**
- **Bottle opening**: DEXOP 12/min vs TeleOP 5/min — **2.4× faster**

**Policy learning success rates (Table 2, bulb installation):**

- 160 DEXOP + 40 TeleOP mixed: **51.3%±3.2%** success in 139 min collection
- 200 TeleOP only: **42.5%±3.2%** success in 283 min collection

Higher success rate achieved in **half the collection time**.

## Achievements and Limitations

**What's newly achieved:**
- Pure mechanical linkage (no actuators, batteries, or calibration) achieves reliable human-to-robot dexterous motion transfer
- Direct contact feedback enables natural manipulation of contact-rich tasks (drilling, bulb installation) that are practically infeasible with teleoperation
- Better policy performance at half the data collection cost vs. teleoperation-only baselines

**Limitations:**
- Per-robot-hand co-design and linkage fabrication required for each new robot platform
- Provides proprioceptive but not tactile feedback to the human operator — subtle surface textures felt by the robot are not conveyed to the human
- Current EyeSight hand lacks full human hand DOF, limiting tasks requiring in-hand reorientation or complex palm contact
- 🤖 Policy evaluation focuses on a single task (bulb installation); it remains unclear whether DEXOP's data quality advantage generalizes uniformly across a broader and more diverse task suite
