## TL;DR

Does pretraining a VLA on 20,854 hours of egocentric human video dramatically improve robotic dexterity? EgoScale discovers a log-linear scaling law (R²=0.9983) between data volume and performance, achieving 54% success rate improvement on a 22-DoF robotic hand and one-shot transfer (88% on shirt folding).

## Problem

**Large-scale utilization of human behavioral data** is the key challenge in robotic dexterous manipulation learning. Robot teleoperation data is expensive to collect and hard to scale, while egocentric human video is abundant online. The questions are: (1) how to extract action labels (hand motions) from human video, (2) how to bridge the human-robot embodiment gap, and (3) whether data scale actually translates to performance gains.

## Key Idea

EgoScale proposes a **two-stage human-to-robot learning framework**:

- **Stage I — Large-scale human pretraining**: Extract wrist motion (SE(3)) + 22-DoF hand joint actions from 20,854 hours of egocentric video to pretrain a flow-based VLA. 100K steps, batch 8,192
- **Stage II — Lightweight aligned mid-training**: Collect aligned human-robot play data using Vive trackers + Manus gloves + cameras. Freeze VLM backbone, train action expert only. 344 tasks, ~50h human + ~4h robot
- **Stage III — Task-specific fine-tuning**: 10K steps, batch 512

<Collapsible title="Detailed Methods">

### Action Representation

- **Wrist motion**: Relative SE(3) transform $\Delta\mathbf{W}^t = (\mathbf{W}_0^w)^{-1}\mathbf{W}_t^w$
- **Hand actions**: Optimization-based retargeting to 22-DoF joint space (outperforms wrist-only and fingertip representations)

### Model Architecture

- Vision-Language backbone (VLM) + DiT action expert
- Embodiment-conditioned MLP adapter for diverse robot hands
- Egocentric RGB + dual wrist cameras as 3-channel input

### Scaling Law

$$L = 0.024 - 0.003 \cdot \ln(D)$$

$L$: validation loss, $D$: pretraining data hours. Log-linear relationship across 1K-20K hour range (R²=0.9983). No saturation observed within explored regime.

</Collapsible>

## Key Results

- **Pretraining effect**: **54% average success rate improvement** over no-pretraining baseline (22-DoF Sharpa hand, 5 tasks)
- **Scaling law**: Log-linear relationship between data scale and performance (**R²=0.9983**) — more data guarantees better performance
- **One-shot transfer**: Single robot demo + aligned human data achieves shirt folding **88%**, bottle unscrewing **55%**
- **Cross-embodiment**: Transfer to Unitree G1 tri-finger hand shows **30%p absolute improvement** (with G1 play data)

## Achievements and Limitations

**Key achievements**
- **20,854 hours** of egocentric human video for robot dexterity pretraining — **20× larger** than prior efforts
- First discovery of **scaling law** for human data → robot performance (R²=0.9983)
- Aligned mid-training enables **one-shot transfer** — learning new tasks from a single demonstration

**Limitations**
- No saturation observed — more data needed but collection cost remains high
- Long-horizon planning may require joint scaling of data and model capacity
- Self-supervised objectives on unlabeled video unexplored
- 🤖 Validated on only 5 lab tasks; generalization to unstructured real environments and sustained operation untested
