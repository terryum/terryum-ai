## TL;DR

What advantages does applying diffusion models to robot behavior cloning yield? Diffusion Policy represents visuomotor policies as conditional denoising diffusion processes, gaining three key benefits — graceful handling of multimodal action distributions, scalability to high-dimensional action spaces, and stable training — achieving an average 46.9% improvement across 15 tasks from 4 benchmarks.

## Problem

Behavior cloning from demonstrations faces three fundamental challenges. First, **multimodal action distributions** — demonstrators may take different actions from the same state — where explicit policies (Gaussian, GMM) suffer from mode averaging and implicit policies (EBMs) from training instability. Second, the need to predict **action sequences** rather than single actions creates high-dimensional output spaces. Third, **training instability** arising from negative sampling in energy-based models.

## Key Idea

Diffusion Policy's core insight is representing robot policies as **DDPMs (Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models)**. It learns the score function (gradient) of the action distribution and iteratively refines noise into actions via stochastic Langevin dynamics during inference.

- **Receding Horizon Control**: Predicts action sequences but replans at each step, achieving both closed-loop execution and temporal action consistency
- **Visual Conditioning**: Treats visual observations as conditioning rather than part of the joint distribution, extracting visual representations once regardless of denoising iterations → enables real-time inference
- **Time-series Diffusion Transformer**: Transformer-based diffusion network that reduces over-smoothing effects of CNN models, suited for tasks requiring rapid action changes and velocity control

### DDPM Training

Training loss:

$$\mathcal{L} = \text{MSE}(\epsilon_k, \epsilon_\theta(x_0 + \epsilon_k, k))$$

Noise prediction network $\epsilon_\theta$ predicts the noise added at denoising iteration k.

### Score Function and EBM Connection

Diffusion Policy learns the score function of the action distribution:

$$\nabla_a \log p(a|o) = -\nabla_a E_\theta(a, o) - \underbrace{\nabla_a \log Z(o, \theta)}_{=0} \approx -\epsilon_\theta(a, o)$$

Independent of the normalization constant $Z(o, \theta)$, fundamentally resolving EBM training instability.

### CNN vs Transformer Backbone

- **CNN-based (DiffusionPolicy-C)**: FiLM conditioning applies observations to every convolution layer. Works well on most tasks without hyperparameter tuning
- **Transformer-based (DiffusionPolicy-T)**: Cross-attention integrates observations. Superior when task complexity and action change rates are high, but more sensitive to hyperparameters

## Key Results

- **Average 46.9% improvement across 15 tasks from 4 benchmarks** vs existing SOTA
- **RoboMimic (State)**: Square task DiffusionPolicy-C 93% (LSTM-GMM: 73%), ToolHang 87% (BET: 20%)
- **RoboMimic (Visual)**: Transport-mh DiffusionPolicy-C 69% (LSTM-GMM: 24%)
- **BlockPush**: DiffusionPolicy-T achieves 94% on 2-block simultaneous push (BET: 71%, IBC: 0%)
- **Kitchen**: DiffusionPolicy-C achieves 99% on 4-stage task (BET: 44%, LSTM-GMM: 34%)
- **Training stability**: IBC's inference accuracy doesn't improve despite decreasing training loss and success rate oscillates, while Diffusion Policy converges stably

## Achievements and Limitations

**Key achievements**
- **First systematic application of diffusion models to robot policies** — consistent SOTA across 4 benchmarks
- **Gracefully handles multimodal action distributions** without mode averaging — commits to a single mode within each rollout
- Receding horizon + visual conditioning enables **real-time closed-loop control**

**Limitations**
- CNN backbone over-smooths rapid action changes; Transformer backbone is hyperparameter-sensitive — backbone selection required per task
- DDPM's iterative denoising limits inference speed — potential bottleneck for high-frequency control requirements
- Highly dependent on demonstration quality — performance bounds with suboptimal demonstrations unexplored
- 🤖 All evaluations focus on relatively simple single-arm manipulation tasks; scalability to bipedal locomotion or complex multi-joint tasks remains unverified
