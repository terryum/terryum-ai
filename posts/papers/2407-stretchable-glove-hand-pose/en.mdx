## TL;DR

Existing hand tracking gloves are limited in pose recognition and require complex multi-sensor setups. This work solves both with a **one-size-fits-all stretchable glove** that simultaneously estimates bone lengths and joint angles from a single sensing mechanism.

## Problem

Existing hand tracking systems either combine multiple sensors (IMUs, encoders, bending sensors) to measure joint angles, or rely on vision-based approaches that are susceptible to camera setup requirements and occlusion. Determining the wearer's hand size (bone lengths) typically requires separate manual measurement or vision-based calibration, which is a major source of error in forward kinematic (FK) reconstruction. Moreover, most prior work evaluated performance on a limited set of common hand poses, leaving robustness on unconventional and complex poses unverified.

## Key Idea

**Simultaneous bone length and joint angle estimation from a single stretchable sensing mechanism**: A soft sensing layer with 9 eGaIn (eutectic gallium-indium) liquid-metal traces is pre-stretched when worn, providing an initial estimate of hand size. Bone lengths are then refined via Bayesian updates using sensor signal distributions during dynamic hand motion, while joint angles are estimated in real time using a Kalman filter-based linear system model.

Key components:

- **Multi-stiffness substrate**: Thickness variation increases sensor sensitivity to a gauge factor of 3.4, implemented with a single material
- **Anchor point design**: Hook-and-loop patches fix sensors to bone midpoints, ensuring an injective mapping between sensor signals and joint angles
- **Comprehensive motion data**: Calibration uses free hand motion data collected with custom motion-capture frames, evaluating across the full range of motion rather than limited poses
- **FK reconstruction**: Estimated bone lengths + joint angles enable constraint-free reconstruction of arbitrary poses

<Collapsible title="Detailed Method">

The system reconstructs hand poses in three main stages.

**Stage 1 — Initial bone length estimation**: When the sensing layer is pre-stretched onto the wearer's hand, the initial resistance values of 9 sensors form a unique pattern depending on hand size. Gaussian Process Regression maps sensor signals → 10 bone lengths using data from 13 subjects. Mean absolute error: 2.06mm (~6.9%).

**Stage 2 — Bayesian bone length refinement**: As the wearer moves their fingers, the statistical distribution of sensor signals varies with hand size. Bayes' rule updates the posterior:

$$\hat{x}_k = \hat{x}_{k-1} + P_{k-1}M^\top(MP_{k-1}M^\top + \hat{\Sigma})^{-1}(y_k - M\hat{x}_{k-1})$$

The mean and variance of sensor signals collected during 8 designated poses are linearly fitted to bone lengths to construct the likelihood function, achieving an additional 8.3% error reduction on average.

**Stage 3 — Real-time joint angle estimation**: A linear system model with state vector $x_k \in \mathbb{R}^{10 \times 6}$ and observation $y_k \in \mathbb{R}^9$ is estimated via Kalman filter:

$$x_{k+1} = Ax_k + w_k, \quad y_k = Hx_k + v_k$$

where $A$ is the state transition matrix, $H$ is the observation matrix, and $w_k$, $v_k$ are process and measurement noise, respectively. Separate calibration per hand size compensates for pre-stretch differences.

## Key Results

- **Bone length estimation error: 2.1mm average** (~6.9% of bone length), with additional 8.3% reduction after Bayesian refinement
- **Joint angle estimation error: 4.16° average**, outperforming commercial hand tracking systems across all hand sizes and calibration conditions
- **Fingertip 3D position error: 4.02mm average** — **less than half** the smallest reported error among commercial systems
- Robust reconstruction demonstrated across 3 applications including virtual number pad typing, shadowgraphy, and Allegro Hand teleoperation with unconventional poses

## Achievements and Limitations

### What's New

- First one-size-fits-all glove system that simultaneously estimates bone lengths and joint angles from a single stretchable sensing mechanism
- Calibration and evaluation using comprehensive free hand motion data, proving FK reconstruction robustness even on unconventional poses (e.g., shadowgraphy)
- Achieved less than half the fingertip position error (4.02mm) of commercial systems, with Allegro Hand teleoperation demonstration

### Limitations

- **3-finger limitation**: Only thumb, index, and middle fingers are directly measured; ring and little fingers are estimated as proportions of the middle finger, preventing independent control
- **Constrained refinement poses**: Bayesian bone length refinement is limited to 8 designated poses; refinement during arbitrary motion is left for future work
- 🤖 **Linear sensor model**: Joint angle estimation uses a linear system model, potentially degrading accuracy at extreme poses where nonlinear sensor responses dominate
